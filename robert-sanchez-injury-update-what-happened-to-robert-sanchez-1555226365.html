Who is Robert Sanchez?

Robert Lynch Sánchez is a 26-year-old Spanish professional footballer known for his role as a goalkeeper. Born on November 18, 1997, in Cartagena, Spain, he currently plays for the Premier League club Chelsea and represents the Spain national team. Standing at an impressive height of 1.97 meters (6 feet 6 inches), Robert has gained recognition for his skills between the goalposts. He started his football journey in the youth ranks, showcasing his talent and eventually making a name for himself in top-tier football.

Robert Sanchez Early life

Born to a Jamaican-English father and a Spanish mother in Cartagena, Robert's early football journey began with local clubs such as Escuela de Fútbol de Santa Ana, Cartagena FC, and Ciudad Jardín. His talent did not go unnoticed, and at the age of 15, he made a significant move to England to join Brighton & Hove Albion. Starting his professional career with Brighton, he signed his first contract in June 2015, marking the beginning of a promising football career.

Robert Sanchez Club Career

Robert Sanchez's club career has been marked by significant milestones. After joining Brighton & Hove Albion at 15, he underwent loan spells at Forest Green Rovers and Rochdale, gaining valuable experience. His Brighton debut came on November 1, 2020, in a Premier League match against Tottenham Hotspur.

Known for his exceptional skills, he signed a new four-and-a-half-year contract with Brighton in February 2021. In August 2023, Robert made a high-profile move to Chelsea for a reported transfer fee of £20 million, solidifying his position as a top goalkeeper.

Robert Sanchez International Career

Robert's international journey with the Spain national team began in March 2021 when he received his first call-up for the 2022 FIFA World Cup qualification matches. He was part of Spain's squad for the delayed UEFA Euro 2020 tournament, contributing to their journey to the semi-final.

Making his international debut on September 5, 2021, in a World Cup qualifier against Georgia, Robert has become a valuable asset for Spain. His inclusion in Spain's squad for the 2022 FIFA World Cup demonstrates his growing stature in international football.
