Who is Gordon Ramsay?

Gordon Ramsay, born on 8th November 1966, is a British celebrity chef, restaurateur, television presenter, and writer. In 1997, he founded Gordon Ramsay Restaurants, which has been awarded 17 Michelin stars in total and currently holds seven. His signature restaurant, Restaurant Gordon Ramsay in London, has held three Michelin stars since 2001. Rising to fame with the British TV show "Boiling Point" in 1999, Ramsay became one of the world's best-known and most influential chefs.

Ramsay's television persona is characterized by his fiery temper, strict demeanor, and frequent use of profanity while delivering blunt, critical, and often controversial comments, including insults and sardonic wisecracks about contestants and their cooking abilities. He is involved in various industries, including television, film, hospitality, and food, and has mentored and promoted numerous chefs who have apprenticed under him. His TV shows include competitive cookery and food-related programs like "Hell's Kitchen," "Ramsay's Kitchen Nightmares," "The F Word," "MasterChef," "MasterChef Junior," "Hotel Hell," and many more.
