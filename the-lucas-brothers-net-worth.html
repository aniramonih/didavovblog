The Lucas Brothers Full Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings and Education

Kenny and Keith Lucas, known collectively as The Lucas Brothers, embarked on an unconventional path to stardom. Born and raised in Newark, New Jersey, with time spent in High Point, North Carolina, they moved to Irvington, New Jersey, and graduated from Irvington High School.

Both brothers graduated with honors from the College of New Jersey in 2007, earning degrees in philosophy. Remarkably, they both attended law school – Kenny at New York University and Keith at Duke University – but in a dramatic turn, they dropped out in the final week of their third year to pursue a career in stand-up comedy.

Breaking into the Comedy Scene

They made their late-night television debut on Late Night With Jimmy Fallon in 2012. This appearance marked the beginning of their rise in the comedy world.

They created the animated series Lucas Bros. Moving Co. and starred in truTV’s Friends of the People. Their unique brand of humor caught the attention of many, leading to their inclusion in Variety magazine’s Ten Comics to Watch in 2014 list.

Hollywood Ventures and Acclaim

The twins’ foray into Hollywood included a role in the feature film 22 Jump Street (2014). They continued to expand their presence on television, appearing in episodes of the Fox TV series The Grinder in 2016 and playing themselves in the Netflix series Lady Dynamite.

Netflix Special and Recognition

In April 2017, they released their first comedy special, On Drugs, on Netflix. The special was well-received, with Paste magazine ranking it as the 9th best comedy special of 2017. Their comedic style, often infused with philosophical and social themes, resonated with a wide audience.

A Milestone with Judas and the Black Messiah

Their career took a significant turn when they co-wrote and co-produced the film Judas and the Black Messiah (2021).

This biopic of Fred Hampton, co-written with Will Berson and directed by Shaka King, earned them nominations for the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay and the Writers Guild of America Award for Best Original Screenplay.

They won the Paul Selvin Award and the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Writing in a Motion Picture, marking a high point in their career.

Ongoing Projects and Collaborations

They continue to develop their career in comedy and film. They are working on a comedy project with Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, who directed 22 Jump Street. Additionally, they are set to write and star in a semi-autobiographical film produced by Judd Apatow.

As regulars at the Comedy Cellar, they remain active in the stand-up comedy scene, constantly evolving their craft.

Who are The Lucas Brothers?

Why did they decide to leave law school for comedy?

They left law school to pursue a career in stand-up comedy, inspired by the freedom and artistic expression they saw in the lives of comedians performing in New York City .
