Maggie McGraw is the second youngest daughter of Tim McGraw and Faith Hill, who hasn't exactly followed in her parents' footsteps like her sisters. The Stanford graduate recently wowed fans in an outfit she curated to channel a historical figure, while others saw the beloved fictional character Captain Jack Sparrow.

In a recent Instagram post, Maggie McGraw shared a bathroom selfie showcasing a head-turning outfit. Rocking a middle-parted bob, the 25-year-old donned a classic black suit with a wide belt secured at her waist and a frilly white shirt. Captioning the photo, she said, "Dirksen senate office building could never."

Garnering a comment section full of compliments, one user said: "This shirt is giving Captain Jack [pirate flag emoji]." In her response, Maggie explained she was channeling the American founding father, Alexander Hamilton. But, resigned to public opinion, she concluded, "but that's fine."

Maggie's sister, Audrey McGraw, also gave her stamp of approval, simply commenting, "Yes," under the post. And while one fan marveled over how she had two phones, singer Sarah Evans also complimented Maggie's haircut, saying she loved the bob hairstyle.

Maggie drew her first breath on August 12, 1998. She attained a Master's degree from Stanford University in 2021 after graduating the previous year with a degree in Climate Control. According to her dad, who described her as a "free diver," Maggie had always been passionate about the environment, especially the sea.

Though she initially kept her social media presence relatively private, Maggie still graced the red carpet alongside her dad at several high-profile events, including the 2016 AMAs and CMT Awards. While studying at Stanford, Maggie was the frontwoman of a girl band with whom she regularly performed.

She was also romantically involved with a college sweetheart named John Whitling. The pair reportedly met at Stanford, and although Whitling kept his social accounts private, he frequently appeared on his girlfriend's timeline. However, at the time of this writing, no posts included John on Maggie's Instagram.

Apart from her romantic life, Maggie has maintained a loving bond with her sisters, Audrey and Gracie McGraw, both of whom joined the entertainment industry alongside their parents. Gracie is an actress who appeared on the TV show "If Loving You Is Wrong" between 2018 and 2020.

She's also a singer who regularly showcases her vocal prowess on social media. Straight out of high school, Audrey started doing high-profile campaigns. Like Gracie, Audrey inherited a lovely voice from her parents. She once shared an impressive piano cover of "Fire and Ice" by Pat Benatar.
