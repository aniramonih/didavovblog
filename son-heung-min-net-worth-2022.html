Son Heung-min Club Career

Son began his football career with FC Seoul at an early age. His father worked hard to educate him and instill in him a sense of ambition and hunger. In 2008, he joined Hamburger SV’s youth setup after breaking through with the South Korean squad.

He was assigned to FC Seoul in the next season. He, on the other hand, saw it as a learning experience rather than a setback. Hamburger SV called him back after he performed admirably. In 2010, he made his senior debut for the German club and went on to play 78 games for them.

Bayer Leverkusen noticed his amazing progress in Germany and signed him in 2013. In just one season at the German club, he secured a starting spot in the squad. In 87 appearances across two years with the club, he scored 29 goals and added 11 assists. During his second season with the German club, he drew strong interest from Premier League club Tottenham Hotspur.

He was signed by the Spurs in 2015. He has been a top performer on the English team since then. He scored four goals and added one assist in his first season.

He began breathing fire from his boot as soon as he relaxed with the crew. In 308 games, he has 117 goals and 69 assists to his credit.

He has already established himself as a major character at the club. Even though he was out for the whole 2021/22 season due to an unexpected injury, he still managed to be the team’s leading scorer.

Son Heung-min International Career

Son has been a part of the national team since he was a teenager. In 2009, he was a participant in the South Korean U-17 men’s football squad that competed in the U-17 World Cup. His outstanding qualities helped him earn his senior debut after only one year. In 2010, he played for the senior team in a friendly match against Syria.

In recent seasons, Son has emerged as a key member of his national team. He represented South Korea in the 2014 and 2018 World Cups. His major break, though, came at the Asian Games. He helped his team win the 2018 Asian Cup, which allowed the entire squad to avoid mandatory military duty.
