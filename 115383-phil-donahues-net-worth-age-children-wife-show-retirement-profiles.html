Who is Phil Donahue?

Phillip John Donahue is a celebrated media personality, writer, film producer, and show host. He revolutionised talk shows by allowing live audience participation. Many contemporary talk show hosts now allow participation from the audience.

How old is Phil Donahue?

Phil Donahue's age is 87 years as of 2023. He was born on 21st December 1935, and his Zodiac sign is Sagittarius.

Is Phil Donahue still alive?

Yes, the talk show host is still alive. Due to his advanced age, he seldom makes media appearances. He is now retired and living a quiet life.

Where is Phil Donahue from?

The show host was born in Cleveland, Ohio, United States of America. He resides in Manhattan, New York City, United States of America. His nationality is American, and his ethnicity is White (Irish).

Who are Phil Donahue's parents?

Phil's father is Phillip Donahue, and his mother is Catherine Donahue (née McClory). His dad was a furniture sales clerk, while his mom was a department store shoe clerk.

He has a sister, Elinor Donahue, renowned for her roles in movies like Father Knows Best. Elinor and her brother were raised in a Catholic home.

Educational background

The film producer went to Our Lady of Angels Elementary School and graduated in 1949. He then proceeded to St. Edward High School, an all-boys private high school. He was in the institution's first graduating class in 1953.

He then joined the University of Notre Dame for higher studies and graduated in 1957 with a Bachelor of Business Administration degree.

Career

The media personality started his career in 1957. He was a production assistant at KYW radio and television in Cleveland, Ohio. One day, an announcer skipped work, so he got the opportunity to stand in for them.

He then served as a bank check sorter in Albuquerque, New Mexico, before becoming a programme director for WABJ radio. He later became a stringer for the CBS Evening News before being appointed the anchor of the morning newscast at WHIO-TV in Dayton, Ohio.

From 1963 to 1967, he hosted Conversation Piece on WHIO radio. Some of the people he interviewed are John F. Kennedy, Johnny Carson, Malcolm X, and Jerry Rubin, among others.

In late 1967, he started hosting The Phil Donahue Show on WLWD (now WDTN). In January 1970, the show entered nationwide syndication.

The show focused on issues that divide liberals and conservatives in the United States of America. These included war issues, termination of pregnancies, consumer protection, and civil rights.

The show moved from Dayton to Chicago in 1974 and to New York City in 1984. His show ran for 29 years before ending on 13th September 1996.

The show was cancelled after its ratings went down. It lost viewers when stations either started dropping it or moving it to late-night and early-morning time slots.

As he hosted the show, Phil also contributed to The Today Show on NBC from 1979 to 1988. In the 1980s, he and Soviet journalist Vladimir Posner co-hosted the U.S.–Soviet Space Bridge.

Donahue on MSNBC

In July 2002, the talk show host returned on air to host Donahue on MSNBC. The show was cancelled on 25th February 2003.

Why did Donahue go off the air?

Donahue was cancelled because the host opposed the imminent American invasion of Iraq. MSNBC felt that he would be a problematic public face for NBC in a time of war.
