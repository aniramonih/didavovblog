How did Dave Bosecker die? Know Everything About this Incident

 Posted on August 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  520 words  |  Billy Koelling

Tragedy struck the Eastland County community on Friday, July 21, 2023, as they mourn the loss of Dave Bosecker, a dedicated police officer. While on duty, Deputy Bosecker lost his life in a shooting at a residence situated along Highway 183, between Cisco and Rising Star. Responding to the distress call, Eastland police officials arrived at the scene only to find the devastating outcome of the incident. The community now comes together to remember the brave officer who made the ultimate sacrifice in service to his community.

Who was Dave Bosecker?

Deputy Bosecker’s legacy in law enforcement spanned an impressive 21 years, during which he earned the esteemed title of a master peace officer. His journey in this noble profession began as a deputy in Wise County, from where he ventured into service with the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission. With a passion for preserving the natural world, he took on the role of a Game Warden for Texas Parks and Wildlife. His dedication to protect and serve led him to become a Police Officer for Comanche PD.

Finally, in 2021, Deputy Bosecker found his place at Eastland County as a deputy, further demonstrating his commitment to safeguarding the community. Not content with just one role, he also contributed his skills part-time to Cisco PD as a marine enforcement officer. His impressive career path and unwavering dedication leave behind a remarkable legacy in the realm of law enforcement.

How did Dave Bosecker die? What was his Cause of death?

On the night of Friday, July 21, 2023, at around nine o’clock, the Eastland County Sheriff’s Office received a distress call regarding a domestic dispute at a home situated on Highway 183 between Cisco and Rising Star. Deputy David Bosecker was the first officer to reach the scene. Tragically, he was immediately met with gunfire from the suspect involved in the dispute. Despite efforts to save him, Deputy Bosecker succumbed to his injuries. The suspect, identified as Cody Douglas Pritchard, has been arrested and taken into custody at Stephens County Jail. He faces charges of Capital Murder of a Peace Officer, reflecting the severity of the alleged crime. As of now, no bond has been established for his release. The investigation is actively underway, and it is being spearheaded by the Texas Rangers, who are working diligently to gather all the facts and evidence related to the case.
