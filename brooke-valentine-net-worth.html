Early Life and Career Beginnings

Brooke Valentine, born Kanesha Nichole Brookes on October 5, 1984, in Houston, Texas, kicked off her career with the girl group Best Kept Secret.

Realizing her dreams were bigger than Texas, she moved to Los Angeles and signed with Virgin Records. Her talent and determination were about to take her places.

Breakthrough with Girlfight

Brooke made waves with her debut single Girlfight, featuring Lil Jon and Big Boi. The song climbed the charts, reaching #23 on the Billboard Hot 100 and #13 on the R&B chart.

Her debut album, Chain Letter, was a hit, debuting at #16 on the Billboard 200. This success marked her entry into the big league.

Career Setbacks and the Unreleased Physical Education

Despite her initial success, Brooke faced setbacks with her second album, Physical Education.

The project was shelved due to a merger between Virgin Records and Capitol Records. However, Brooke didn’t give up. She independently released the Physical Education Mixtape in 2009, showcasing her resilience.

Transition to B. Valentine and Comeback

After a hiatus, during which she focused on her personal life and became a mother to her son London, Brooke returned to the music scene.

She released new singles and an EP titled Love Letters. Adopting the stage name B. Valentine, she made a strong comeback.
