Patrice Bergeron wife Stephanie Bertrand is a Laval University graduate in Psychology. Patrice and Stephanie married on 20 July 2013. 

Patrice is a professional ice hockey player and the captain of the Boston Bruins of the NHL. The Boston Bruins selected him with the 45th overall pick during the 2003 NHL Entry Draft.

He scored his first hat-trick in the NHL in the 2011 Bruins' victory over the Ottawa Senators and was named NHL's First Star of the Month.

Bergeron received the NHL Foundation Player Award and the second Frank J. Selke Trophy for his efforts during the regular season.

He was selected to represent Canada at the 2004 World Championships in Prague. He made his international debut and won his first gold medal.

Patrice was the first to win a men's gold medal before winning at the junior level by helping Canada win the competition.

Patrice Bergeron Wife

Patrice Bergeron and Stephanie Bertrand have been together for seventeen years. Patrice and Stephanie started dating in 2006.

The couple dated for seven years and got married in 2013. They are very supportive of each other.

Stephanie joins Patrice's games and she also attends award events with him. Heavy reported Stephanie was on her husband's side when he received the Selke trophy in 2017 and the two of them smiled together.

In the award speech, Patrice thanked Bertrand for always being there for him and called her a great mom.

Stephanie and his children were present for the pre-game ceremony where the Boston Bruins honored Patrice for reaching 1,000 NHL points.

Who is Stephanie Bertrand?

Stephanie Bertrand is the wife of the professional ice hockey player Patrice Bergeron. Stephanie graduated from Laval University in Quebec City.

She spends the hockey season in Boston and the summers in Quebec. She and her husband bought a house in Saint-Augustin-de-Desmaures in central Quebec on the Saint Lawrence River.

Stephanie is a very supportive mother. She enjoys spending time with her family and exploring new destinations. 

She attended the NHL award with her husband at Mandalay Bay Resort and Casino on June 19, 2019, in Las Vegas, Nevada. 

Patrice Bergeron Children

Patrice Bergeron and Stephanie Bertrand have three kids, two sons Zack and Noah Bergeron and one daughter Victoria Bergeron.

Patrice has talked about his parenthood in a Mass Mutual blog post. He said that now that he is a parent, he and Stephanie have made family their top priority in their home.

He claimed that the sacrifices his parents made for him and his sibling now affect him as a parent. Like Patrice's parents supported him, he also wants to support his children.

Inside Patrice Bergeron Family Life

Patrice Bergeron grew up in Quebec, Canada with his family. Patrice grew up playing hockey following the footsteps of his brother Guillaume Bergeron.

His mother Sylvie Bergeron and father Gerard have always supported and encouraged him in his career.

The Quebec Major Junior Hockey League selected him at the age of 17 and gave him a chance to meet NHL scouts. His father and mother supported him and took him long distances for practice.

His Mother Sylvie Bergeron Is Social Worker

Patrice mother Sylvie worked as a social worker as she had a passion for helping others. 

She helped those who had suffered head trauma and injuries. She quit her job to be at home with her sons when they entered their adolescent years when academics and hockey grew more demanding.

Sylvie made a selfish decision and his son Patrice appreciate it today also. 

Gerard Cleary Is A Proud Father

Patrice Bergeron Dad Gerard Cleary was a foreman for a city. In 2023, he is battling cancer per reports.

During the Stanley Cup playoffs before sustaining an injury, he wanted to play in presence of his dad in Montreal.

He put a lot of effort into paying for his sons' expensive hockey equipment and related costs. He used to take his sons to different friends' homes and was always there for them.

Patrice stated that his parents made sure that he and his brother learned how to communicate and healthily process their emotions.
