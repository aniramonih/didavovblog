Giannis Antetokounmpo height- Giannis Sina Ugo Antetokounmpo is a Greek-Nigerian professional basketball player for the Milwaukee Bucks of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

The son of immigrants from Nigeria, Giannis Sina Ugo Adetokunbo was born on December 6, 1994, in Athens, Greece. His parents had left Lagos three years prior, leaving their firstborn son, Francis, in the custody of his grandparents.

Sepolia, an area of Athens, was the place where Adetokunbo was raised. Giannis and his older brother Thanasis sold watches, purses, and sunglasses in the streets to support their parents, who struggled to obtain employment due to their immigration status. Adetokunbo began playing basketball in 2007.

During the 2011–12 season, Antetokounmpo participated with the senior men’s squad of Filathlitikos in the semi-pro Greek B Basket League (Third Division).

A month or so after turning 18 in December 2012, Antetokounmpo agreed to a four-year contract with the Spanish team CAI Zaragoza, which reportedly included NBA buyouts at the end of each season. Other top European teams, like Barcelona and Anadolu Efes, were interested in signing him.

Antetokounmpo formally qualified for the 2013 NBA draft on April 28, 2013. He was taken by the Milwaukee Bucks as the 15th overall choice, which was in line with his draft forecasts as a first-round pick. He signed his rookie scale contract with the Bucks on July 30, 2013.

At the 2013 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship, Antetokounmpo made his debut as Greece’s representative.

He was a member of the Greek Under-20 national team. He averaged 8.0 points, 7.6 rebounds, and 2.2 assists over the course of the 10 games as he assisted Greece to an 8-2 record and a fifth-place overall finish. He placed second in defensive rebounds (7.0) and eighth in shot blocks at the end of the competition (1.4).

Antetokounmpo made his debut for the senior men’s Greek national basketball team in 2014, contributing to their 5-1 record and ninth-place finish at the FIBA Basketball World Cup. Over the course of the six games, he averaged 6.3 points and 4.3 rebounds while shooting 45.8% from the floor.

Antetokounmpo has drawn recognition for his ability to set up plays. He has primarily directed the Bucks’ offense despite not being the team’s primary ball handler.

One of the highest rates in the league, Antetokounmpo was accountable for 57.8% of the Bucks’ points scored when he was on the court in the 2019–20 season (via both his own scoring and assists). In both the 2018-19 and 2019-20 seasons, he contributed nearly 6 assists per game.
