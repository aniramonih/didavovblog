Early Life & Biography

Ethel Kennedy was born in Chicago, Illinois, USA, on 11 April 1928. Her father,- George Skakel, was a businessman and a Protestant of Dutch origin, while her mother- Ann Brannack was a secretary and a Catholic of Irish descent. Unfortunately, both of Ethel’s parents died in a plane crash in 1955.

She was the 3rd of the four daughters and the 6th of the seven children in the family, where her five older siblings were Georgeann(1918-1983), James(1921-1998), George Jr.(1922-1966), Rushton(1923-2003) and Patricia(1925-2000) and her one younger sister who is still alive, is Ann(b. 1933). She went to an all-girls Greenwich Academy located in Greenwich, Connecticut- a place where she and her other siblings, raised Catholic.

She graduated later on in 1945, from the Convent of the Sacred Heart in Manhattan. In September of that very year, she began with her college education at Manhattanville College.

Personal Life

It was in February of 1950 when Robert F. Kennedy and Ethel Kennedy got engaged, and then later on 17 June 1950; they got happily married at St. Mary’s Catholic Church in Greenwich, Connecticut. Her husband- Mr. Kennedy worked for the federal government in various investigatory roles as a counsel, for the U.S. Senate.

Ethel Kennedy, along with her husband Robert F. Kennedy, had a lot of lively and ecstatic gatherings at their home, where guests were invited from a broad-diversity and all walks of life. She and her husband had a total of 11 kids together, namely- Kathleen, Joseph, Robert Jr., David, Courtney, Michael, Kerry, Christopher, Max, Douglas, and Rory.

The one incident which shook her life for once and forever took place shortly after midnight on 5 June 1968, when her dear husband Mr. Robert Kennedy was brutally and mortally wounded by a man named Sirhan Sirhan, and unfortunately died early on just the next day.

The president of USA that time- Lyndon B. Johnson declared that day to be a national day of mourning. The poor wife- Ethel Kennedy, from whom suddenly her love was snatched away, was so shaken and shocked after the assassination took place, that she publicly stated that she wouldn’t be remarrying ever again.

Their last child Rory was born after her father tragically left the world forever.
