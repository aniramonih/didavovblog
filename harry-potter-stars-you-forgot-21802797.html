The Harry Potter stars you forgot were engaged and what they've been doing since they split up

 Posted on August 15, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  693 words  |  Tandra Barner

Unbelievably, its 20 years since the first Harry Potter film hit our screens.

We saw all the Harry Potter actors and actress grow up before our eyes playing the iconic characters we know and love.

After all that time, surely there is no info that could emerge that would surprise us.

Read more: The cast member you never knew was related to Voldemort star in real life

But it turns out, there are two actors who got engaged after meeting on the set of the Harry Potter films.

Bonnie Wright, 32, who played Ginny Weasley, started dating Jamie Campbell Bower, 32, when they met when filming The Deathly Hallows Part 1.

Jamie appeared as a young Gellert Grindelwald in the 2010 installment Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1.

He reprised the same role in the 2018 film Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald, becoming the only actor to appear in both film franchises.

However he is also famous for playing the vampire Caius Volturi in the film The Twilight Saga: New Moon and Breaking Dawn films.

In February 2010,Jamie was confirmed to be dating Bonnie after they struck up a romance on set.

Months into their romance, there were reports that the Twilight star Jamie had got down on one knee.

In April 2011, they confirmed their engagement.

"I am engaged. I’m very happy. It’s a wonderful, wonderful time," Jamie told news website The Daily at the time.

However, sadly, on 30 June 2012, they amicably ended their engagement.

But since splitting up, they have both been pretty busy.

Jamie went on to briefly date British-American actress Lily Collins - well-known for Netflix show 'Emily in Paris'.

In his career, Jamie, born in London, began playing the role of Joe, in the West End musical Bend It Like Beckham in 2015.

As for Bonnie, who was born in Tower Hamlets, she's tried her hand at directing.

In 2012, she made her directorial debut with the student film, Separate We Come, Separate We Go, which follows a 10-year-old girl meeting and becoming friends with a widowed man (played by David Thewlis from "Harry Potter.)

Bonnie is an active spokesperson for environmentalism, especially when it comes to plastic.

She joined Greenpeace in 2017 and continues to fight for a cleaner earth through social media by promoting recycled products, showing the beauty of nature, and encouraging followers to take action.

She told US Weekly: “I’ve always been, even from a young age, someone who’s always very interested in taking part in any opportunity I could. Showing up and getting involved in things. So I think that kind of curiosity and eagerness was something that started from a young age,”

"But when it comes to the climate, I had this growing concern and sort of understanding of the amount of single-use plastics that was ending up in our ocean. And the ocean as an environment has always been something that I’ve loved and gotten so much joy from."
