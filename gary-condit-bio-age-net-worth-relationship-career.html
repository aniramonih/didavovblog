Gary Condit, sometimes known as Gary Adrian Condit, was a former American politician who represented California’s 18th congressional district in the House of Representatives from 1989 to 2003.

His adulterous connection with Chandra Levy, a Federal Bureau of Prisons intern, which became public after Levy’s kidnapping in May 2001 and the ultimate discovery of Levy’s remains a year later, helped him gain national attention.

Where was Gary Condit born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Gary Condit was born on April 21, 1948, in Salina, Oklahoma, a city in the United States’ south-central region. Adrian Burl Condit, a Baptist minister, is his father, and Velma Jean Condit is his mother.

His mother passed away in 2017. He was born in Oklahoma and reared there. He graduated from Nathan Hale High School in Tulsa, Oklahoma.

During the summers of his high school years, Condit worked as a warehouseman in Oklahoma’s oil fields. Condit’s family relocated in 1967 when his father was appointed pastor of a Baptist church in Ceres, California, near Modesto. Condit graduated from Modesto Junior College with an Associate of Arts degree in 1970.

In 1972, he received his bachelor’s degree in arts from California State University in Stanislaus, California. Throughout his college years and early career, Condit worked in a tomato cannery, a company that manufactured weapons for the Vietnam War, and the paint department of a Montgomery Ward department store.

Is Gary Condit Married? Relationship

At the age of 18, Gary Condit married Carolyn Berry, his high school lover, in 1967. His daughter Cadee Condit Gray and son Chad Condit also go by these names. A journalistic inquiry in 2001 found that Condit submitted the erroneous birthday on his marriage certificate.

How did Gary Condit start his Professional Career?

Gary Condit has served on the Ceres city council since 1972, previously serving as mayor for two years. He became the city’s youngest mayor at the age of 25, a first in history.

In 1988, he was one of the Gang of Five candidates, along with Chula Vista’s Steve Peace, Los Banos’ Rusty Areias, Rialto’s Gerald R. Eaves, and Whittier’s Charles M. Calderon, who failed to unseat Willie Brown as Speaker of the State Assembly by striking a deal with Republicans.

In the 1988 film Return of the Killer Tomatoes, which Peace co-wrote and directed, Condit made an unexpected, non-speaking appearance during a battle scene.

Condit left politics and relocated to Arizona, where he and his family opened two Baskin-Robbins ice cream stores. Condit was forced to pay the firm $98,000 in a contract dispute, and the company declared bankruptcy. In 2012, he was named chairman of the Phoenix Institute of Desert Agriculture.
