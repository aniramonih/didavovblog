The 7 Little Johnstons is a US television series that is about an average family in America that comprises of seven members. They work and live in rural areas of Forsyth, Georgia, to achieve their dreams all by themselves. They are not an ordinary family but are the largest recognized family of dwarfs (little people) throughout the world.

All these seven Johnstons are diagnosed to have Achondroplasia Dwarfism. It is a genetic condition that impacts the size of their body. The family comprised of five children below the age of sixteen, and their Dad named Trent, and mom, named Amber. Juggling the family demands while living in their newly renovated dream home, Johnstons have a pretty challenging life. The story tells how they overcome these challenges with their determination and persistence.

Evolution

The couple, Amber and Trent Johnston have Achondroplasia dwarfism. They got married and gave birth to two biological children, Elizabeth, and Jonah, who was also diagnosed with a similar condition. Understanding the problems linked with dwarfism, they decided to adopt three more children with the same abnormality. Their adopted children are Alex from South Korea, Anna from Russia, and Emma from China.
