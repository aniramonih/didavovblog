Born on May 4, 1989, Rory McIlroy is a Northern Irish professional golfer who turned pro at 18 and has since become one of the world’s most successful players.

His extraordinary power off the tee and impeccable accuracy made him win the Dubai Desert Classic and the Wells Fargo Championship.

Rory’s impressive golfing career has seen him secure four major championships, which include the 2011 U.S. Open, the 2014 Open Championship, and the 2012 and 2014 PGA Championship.

McIlroy was awarded the Member of the Order of the British Empire (MBE) in 2012 for his remarkable contributions to the sport of golf.

Rory Mcllroy Family Tree and Ethnicity

According to FAMECHAIN, the world-renowned professional golfer was born to a Scottish Father, Gerry McIlroy, and an Irish mother, Rosie McIlroy, in Holywood, Northern Ireland.

Rory McIlroy possesses a British passport; however, he identifies as Northern Irish, British, and Irish altogether. 

Gerry McIlroy, his Father, worked as a bar manager while Rosie McIlroy, his mother, worked in a factory. He shares a great bond with his parents.

His grandfather, Jimmy McIlroy, a renowned Northern Irish footballer, significantly influenced his life by introducing him to sports during his early years.

His family tree also includes his great-uncle Joe McIlroy, who was also a well-known footballer in Northern Ireland.

The MBE has three uncles and one aunt from his Father’s side, namely Colm McIlroy, Brian McIlroy, Mickey McDonald, and Leanne McDonald.

From his mother’s side, no information about any aunts or uncles is available. However, it is known that the athlete has one cousin named Fergus McIlroy.

As mentioned above, he has married his wife, Erica Stoll, who has given birth to Rory McIlroy daughter, Poppy Kennedy McIlroy.

Overall, Rory McIlroy has a supportive family with a strong sporting background, with several members excelling in different sports.
