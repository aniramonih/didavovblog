Professional Career, Movies and Series of Acgtress Daphne Mazwell Reid

Daphne Maxwell Reid began her modeling career while attending university. She also signed with the Eileen Ford Modeling Agency. As a model, she began to feature in various magazines. She is also the first black woman to grace the cover of Glamour Magazine.

Career in Acting

Her acting career began in 1979, when she appeared in two episodes of the CBS animated series “The Duke.” During the same year, she played Dr. Karla Meredith in the television series “A Man Called Sloane.”

She also starred in television shows such as “Coach of the Year” (1980), “WKRP in Cincinnati” (1980-1982), “Hill Street Blues” (1981-1986), “T.J. Hooker” (1983), “Hardcastle and McCormick” (1983), and “The A-Team” (1983-1985).

She was a series regular in the crime drama series “Simon & Simon” as Temple Hill, appearing in a total of 16 episodes. Although she moved to the big screen after making an impression on the small screen with her parts.

She made her film debut as Helene in the comedy film “Protocol” in 1984. In addition, she continued to star in the television programs “The Duck Factory,” “Matt Houston,” and “Paper Dolls” in 1984. She acted in three films in 1985: “The Insiders,” “Cagney & Lacey,” and “The Fall Guy.”

She later appeared in “That’s My Mama Now!” (1986) and “The Long Journey Home” (1987) for television. In the meanwhile, she participated in six episodes of the anthology series “CBS Summer Playhouse” beginning in 1987.

She went on to play Hanna Griffin, a series regular on the comedy-drama series “Frank’s Place.” She also appeared in 22 episodes between 1987 and 1988. During the same time period, she was cast in a recurring role in 18 episodes of the TV series “Super Password.”

Breakthrough performance

She was cast in the 1992 documentary film “Color Adjustment.” Her breakout role, however, was as the substitute actress for Vivian Banks on the NBC comedy “The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air” in 74 episodes from 1993 to 1996, following Janet Hubert-Whitten’s departure.

She later played Miss Maxey in the 1996 period drama film “Once Upon a Time… When We Were Colored,” as well as serving as the film’s second unit director. She played Eartha in the comedy-drama sitcom “Linc’s” from 1998 to 2000, starring in 14 episodes. She appeared in the 2002 stand-up comedy film “Paul Mooney: Analyzing White America” as well as co-executive produced it.

In the years since, she has appeared in several TV shows, including “Eve” (2003-2006), “Crossing Jordan” (2004), “Slavery and the Making of America” (2005), “E! True Hollywood Story” (2007), and “Let’s Stay Together” (2011-2014). She portrayed Elderly Hattie Mae in the 2022 film “A Jazzman’s Blues.” Since that same year, she has appeared as Janice in the drama series “Bel-Air.”
