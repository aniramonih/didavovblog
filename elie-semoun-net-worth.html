Élie Semoun Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Background

Born as Élie Semhoun in France, Semoun’s life was marked by a rich cultural heritage, being of Sephardic Jewish, Moroccan-Jewish, and Algerian-Jewish descent. His early years were shaped by the loss of his mother and the influence of his father, Paul Semhoun.

Early Beginnings

Semoun’s career in entertainment began in 1980 when, at the age of 17, he wrote two collections of poems and two plays. This early foray into writing set the stage for his later success in comedy and acting.

Television Breakthrough

In 1988, Semoun made his television debut with regular appearances on the TF1 series Vivement lundi!, where he played a unique character – a horse mounted on rollers. This role showcased his comedic talent and opened doors for future opportunities.

Rise to Fame with Dieudonné

The pivotal moment in Semoun’s career came in 1990 when he partnered with Dieudonné M’bala M’bala. Together, they wrote and performed daring skits, often addressing taboo subjects like racism and poverty. Their first show at Café de la Gare in 1991 marked the beginning of a successful partnership.

They gained notoriety in 1992 after several appearances on the show Emission Impossible and continued their success with performances at Le Splendid Theater, Paris’s Palais des glaces, and Casino de Paris. The duo, known as Élie and Dieudonné, became one of the most popular comic acts of the 1990s.

Solo Career and Continued Success

The duo split in 1997 due to artistic and financial differences. Semoun then embarked on a solo career, continuing his success with Petites Annonces d’Élie, initially intended to be a show with Dieudonné.

He collaborated with actor Franck Dubosc, and their performances were promoted on the show Les Enfants de la télé (France). The Petites Annonces d’Élie skits, inspired by actual classified ads, featured the character of Cyprian, a repulsive-looking man searching for a busty blond.

Stage Performances and Political Involvement

Semoun returned to the stage with his one-man show, Élie and Semoun. He supported Bertrand Delanoë during Paris’s municipal elections in 2001 and Lionel Jospin during the presidential election of 2002.

In 2003, he released an album of French songs titled Chansons. In early 2005, he performed in a show co-authored with Franck Dubosc and Muriel Robin, titled Élie Semoun se prend pour qui?

Later Works and Albums

Semoun released an album entitled Sur le fil (On the Edge) on March 19, 2007, featuring guest stars like Alexandre Astier and Bérénice Bejo. The skits, written by Semoun and Dubosc, were assisted by comedian Manu Payet.
