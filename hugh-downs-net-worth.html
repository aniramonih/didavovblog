Hugh Downs Finance Overview

Major Television Roles and Their Financial Impact

Hugh Downs is famous for his TV roles. Hosting the Today show from 1962 to 1971 brought him significant wealth. He co-anchored 20/20 from 1978 to 1999, which was another big income source.

He also hosted the game show Concentration from 1958 to 1969, which also contributed to his financial success.

Early Career and Initial Earnings

He started in radio. He worked at WLOK in Lima, Ohio, right after starting college. He then moved to WWJ in Detroit. His first TV job was at WBKB-TV in Chicago. These roles helped build his early wealth.

Significant Broadcasting Achievements

Downs’ significant broadcasting achievements include holding the Guinness World Record for the most hours on commercial network television at one point.

This accolade highlights his dedication and longevity in the industry. His long-term roles on shows like The Tonight Show and Today contributed to his financial success and solidified his legacy in broadcasting.

Earnings from Public Service and Political Views

He served the United Nations and UNICEF. He did public service announcements and voiced strong political views. These roles added to his income, though broadcasting was his main source.
