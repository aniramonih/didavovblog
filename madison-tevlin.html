Meet Actor Madison Tevlin, Who Played Cosentino in Champions

Madison Tevlin (born on December 6, 2001) is a 21-year-old Canadian singer and actor from Toronto who breaks stereotypes about people with Down’s Syndrome. Tevlin first came into the limelight in 2015 because of a viral video in which she could be seen singing John Legend’s “All of Me.”

People were shocked when they heard Maddie singing so beautifully despite her condition because those with Down’s Syndrome usually face problems while speaking.

More than 8 million people watched her video, and now she inspires others. In 2016 she appeared twice on TV first in “Mr. D” and a few months later in “Lost & Found Music Studios.”

In March 2023, Tevlin became a movie star after playing Cosentino in “Champions.” It is a Bobby Farrell-directed film where Woody Harrelson plays the role of a basketball coach whom the court orders to train a team of intellectually challenged players.

Who are Madison Tevlin’s Parents?

On December 6, 2001, Madison Tevlin (age 21) was born to her father, Matt, and mother, Grace Tevlin, in Toronto, Canada. He is the eldest child at home and has a younger brother named Jonny. Growing up with Down’s Syndrome was tough for Madison, but her unshakable will helped her beat all the difficulties.

She developed a love for music when she was six and used to sing regularly for her parents. In 2013 Maddie’s parents decided to send her to a music school so that she could work on her passion.

According to Down Syndrome Education International (DSEI), people who usually face the same condition as Tevlin face difficulty while singing because they have to put more effort than an average person; it is astonishing that the Toronto girl is singing so beautifully.

She is a source of inspiration for those who cry over little things or those who give up their dreams just because of a few challenges. In 2019 she graduated from Loretto College School and received Trustee D’Amico Academic Excellence in Special Education Award.
