Johnny Keating Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Musical Beginnings

Johnny Keating‘s journey began in Edinburgh, Scotland. From an early age, he showed a keen interest in music, mastering both piano and trombone.

Keating’s natural talent was evident as he taught himself to arrange and compose music, setting the stage for a vibrant musical career.

The Big Band Era and Ted Heath

Keating’s career took a pivotal turn when he joined British big band leader Ted Heath in 1952. Initially a trombonist, Keating quickly showcased his diverse talents, prompting Heath to appoint him as his primary arranger just two years into his tenure.

This role elevated Keating’s status in the music world, marking him as a significant figure in the British Big Band era.

Pop Music and Eden Kane

The early 1960s saw Keating’s influence expand into the pop music scene. Alongside songwriter Johnny Worth, Keating was instrumental in shaping the career of British pop star Eden Kane.

A Pioneer in Music Technology

Keating was not just a musician but an innovator. His work with London Records’ Phase 4 series is particularly notable for its pioneering use of synthesizer technology, including the Moog Synthesizer and the EMS VCS 3.

These albums, acclaimed for their high fidelity, were a testament to Keating’s forward-thinking approach to music production.

Lounge Music and Breakbeats

Interestingly, Keating’s influence reached beyond his lifetime. The lounge music revival of the mid-1990s saw a resurgence in the popularity of his work, particularly for its use in breakbeats.

This revival highlighted the timeless quality of Keating’s music, ensuring his legacy in the music world.

Theme from Z-Cars and Football

One of Keating’s most enduring legacies is the Theme from Z-Cars, which became a hit in the UK and was adopted by Everton F.C. as their theme song. This melding of music and sports culture underscores Keating’s widespread influence.

Film Scores and His Musical Range

Keating’s talents also extended to the silver screen. He composed scores for films such as Hotel (1967), Robbery (1967), and Innocent Bystanders (1972). These compositions showcased his ability to adapt his musical style to different narratives and genres.

Educator and Author

Keating’s passion for music was also evident in his educational endeavors. He founded the Johnny Keating School of Music in Edinburgh, shaping the careers of many successful musicians.

Additionally, his academic book, Principles of Songwriting, is a testament to his dedication to music education.

FAQs about Johnny Keating

Who was Johnny Keating?

He was a Scottish musician, arranger, and composer known for his work in jazz, classical, and easy listening genres. He worked with notable artists like Tony Bennett and Bing Crosby and was famous for composing the Theme from Z-Cars.

When and where was he born?

He was born on September 10, 1927, in Edinburgh, Scotland.

What significant achievements did Keating have in his career?

Keating’s career included arranging for major artists like Tony Bennett and Bing Crosby, composing film scores for Hollywood movies such as Hotel and Robbery, and pioneering work with Moog synthesizers in the 1970s.

What is Keating’s most famous work?

Although Keating worked with many famous artists, he is perhaps best known for the Theme from Z-Cars, a top 10 hit from 1962 that became the anthem for Everton FC.

Did he write any books?

Yes, in 1999, Keating completed a four-volume academic reference book titled Principles of Songwriting: A Study in Structure and Technique, dedicated to the art of professional songwriting.

What are some songs written by Keating?

Keating’s songwriting credits include A Little Waltzin‘, Barber Shop Jump by Ted Heath, Come Live With Me by Carmen McRae, and Theme from ‘Hotel’ by Nancy Wilson.

Did he work on any film scores?

Yes, Keating wrote scores for several films, including Hotel (1967) and Robbery (1967), showcasing his versatility as a composer.

What genres did Johnny Keating’s music cover?

His music spans across Jazz, Classical, and Easy Listening genres, with styles including Film Score and Orchestral/Easy Listening.

When did Johnny Keating pass away?

He died in London, England, on May 28, 2015, at the age of 87.

What was Keating’s impact on British jazz?

He was instrumental in the development of a third front in London for big band jazz, distinct from the American East and West Coast schools of jazz. He produced influential albums like British Jazz (1956) and Swinging Scots (1957), contributing significantly to the British jazz scene.
